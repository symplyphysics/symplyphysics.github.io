Classical isochoric molar heat capacity of solids¶

The Dulong-Petit law states that the classical expression for the molar specific heat capacity of certain chemical elements is constant for temperatures far from the absolute zero.

Notation:

  1. \(R\) (R) is molar_gas_constant.

Conditions:

  1. The temperature of the system is big enough to disregard quantum effects.

Links:

  1. Wikipedia.

isochoric_molar_heat_capacity¶

molar_heat_capacity at constant pressure.

Symbol:

c_pm

Latex:

\(c_{p, \text{m}}\)

Dimension:

energy/(amount_of_substance*temperature)

law¶

c_pm = 3 * R

Latex:
\[c_{p, \text{m}} = 3 R\]
