Corrected planet period squared is proportional to cube of semimajor axis¶

Kepler’s third law of planetary motion relates the planet’s rotation period to the semi-major axis of its orbit. Kepler’s laws use the assumption that the attracting mass is much greater than that of the orbiting planet. If this assumption isn’t made, the first and the second law stay the same, but the third law requires a revision. As a result, the period of the planet’s rotation depends not only on the attracting mass, but also on the mass of the planet itself.

law¶

T^2 = (4 * pi^2 * a^3) / (G * (M + m))
