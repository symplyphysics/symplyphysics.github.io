Absolute magnitude of stars from apparent magnitude and distance¶

Absolute magnitude can be calculated using apparent magnitude and distance to the object.

absolute_magnitude¶

Absolute magnitude for stars is defined as the apparent magnitude of an object if it were located at a distance of 10 parsecs (2.063e+6 astronomical units) from the observer and would not experience either interstellar or atmospheric absorption.

Symbol

M

apparent_magnitude¶

The apparent magnitude is a measure of the brightness of a celestial body (more precisely, the illumination created by this body) from the observer’s point of view. The brighter the object, the smaller its magnitude.

Symbol

m

distance¶

Distance to the object.

Symbol

d

distance_constant¶

Constant equal to 2.063e+6 astronomical units.

Symbol

d0

law¶

M = m - 5 * lg(d / d0)

Latex

\(M = m - 5 * \log_{10}(d / d0)\)
