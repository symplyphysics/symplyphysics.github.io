Instantaneous power is dot product of force and velocity¶

Instantaneous power is a measure of the rate of energy transfer or conversion at a given point in time.

Conditions:

  1. Force \(\vec F\) is constant.

Links:

  1. Wikipedia.

power¶

Instantaneous power due to force.

Symbol:

P

Latex:

\(P\)

Dimension:

force¶

Vector of force exerted on the body.

Symbol:

F

Latex:

\({\vec F}\)

Dimension:

force

velocity¶

Vector of the body’s velocity. See speed.

Symbol:

v

Latex:

\({\vec v}\)

Dimension:

velocity

law¶

P = dot(F, v)

Latex:
\[P = \left( {\vec F}, {\vec v} \right)\]
