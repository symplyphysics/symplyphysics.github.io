Current from voltage and diode constant in vacuum diode¶

The current-voltage characteristic of a vacuum diode is described by the \(3/2\)-power law (referred to as Child’s law or Child—Langmuir law). The diode constant in this law depends only on the relative position, shape and size of the electrodes of the vacuum diode.

Conditions:

  1. Electrons travel ballistically between electrodes, i.e. without scattering.

  2. In the interelectrode region, the space charge of any ions is negligible.

  3. The electrons have zero velocity at the cathode surface.
