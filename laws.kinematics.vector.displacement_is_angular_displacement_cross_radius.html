Linear displacement is angular displacement cross radius¶

Assuming a body rotating around a fixed axis, the vector of its linear displacement can be expressed as the cross product of the pseudovector of angular displacement and the radius vector of rotation.

Conditions:

  1. The axis is fixed.

  2. Angular displacement pseudovector and radius vector must be orthogonal to one another.

Links:

  1. Physics LibreTexts, formula 11.1.4.

rotation_radius_vector¶

Radius vector pointing away from the rotation axis perpendicular to it. See distance_to_axis.

Symbol:

\({\vec r}\)

Dimension:

length
