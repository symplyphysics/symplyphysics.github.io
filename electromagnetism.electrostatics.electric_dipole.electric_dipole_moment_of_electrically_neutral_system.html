Electric dipole moment of electrically neutral system¶

The electric dipole moment of an electrically neutral system of (point) charges can be found as the sum of the products of the values and the position vectors of the charges that compose the system.

Notes:

  1. This law can be seen as the definition of electric dipole moment.

  2. The value of the electric dipole moment for such a system is independent of the choice of the origin of the coordinate frame (i.e. it is translationally invariant).

Conditions:

  1. The system is electrically neutral.

Links:

  1. Wikipedia, derivable from the third equation.

law¶

p = Sum(q[i] * r[i], i)

Latex:

electric_neutrality_condition¶

Sum(q[i], i) = 0

Latex:
\[\sum_i {q}_{i} = 0\]
