Straight Wire
=============

If a current is flowing through a **straight wire**, this generates a magnetic field that is 
radially symmetric around said wire and is easier to study than a wire of arbitrary shape. For 
instance, the magnetostatic force between two parallel wires is linearyly proportional to the 
currents that flow through them, which is similar to the formula for the electrostatic force 
between two charges (the only difference is in the power which the distance between them is raised
to).
