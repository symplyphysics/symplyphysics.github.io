Acoustic Waves (Waves)
======================

Acoustic waves are types of mechanical waves that propagate through fluids and solids by causing the
particles of the medium to displace from their equilibrium positions. In fluids, they propagate in a
*longitudinal* manner, causing particles to move along the direction of wave propagation. In solids,
an additional *transverse* component is added due to the presence of *shear moduli*, see
:ref:`Stress (Elasticity)`.

Also see :ref:`Acoustic Waves (Continuum Mechanics)` for the laws regarding the speed of sound in
media.
