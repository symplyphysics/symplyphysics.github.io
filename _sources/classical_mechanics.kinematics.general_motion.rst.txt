General Motion (Kinematics)
===========================

In general, the motion of a classical rigid body can be described at any time as a combination of a
:ref:`linear translation <Translational Motion (Kinematics)>` and a
`rotational motion <Rotational Motion (Kinematics)>`_ around an instantaneous axis of rotation.

As such, the notions of *tangential and normal acceleration* can be defined for any point along the
trajectory of the body, as well as the instantaneous angular velocity of the body can be related to
its instantaneous linear velocity.
