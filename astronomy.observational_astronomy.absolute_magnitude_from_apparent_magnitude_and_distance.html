Absolute magnitude from apparent magnitude and distance¶

Absolute magnitude can be calculated using apparent magnitude and distance to the object.

Conditions:

  1. The extinction from gas and dust, i.e. the interstellar absorption and scattering, is negligible.

Links:

  1. Wikipedia, second formula.

distance_constant¶

Constant equal to \(2.063 \cdot 10^6\) astronomical units.

Symbol:

Latex:

Dimension:

length

law¶

M = m - 5 * log(d / d_0, 10)

Latex:
\[M = m - 5 \log_{10} \left( \frac{d}{d_0} \right)\]
