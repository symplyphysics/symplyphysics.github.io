Isobaric molar heat capacity of ideal gas via adiabatic index¶

For ideal gases the isobaric and isochoric heat capacities can be calculated via the molar gas constant and the adiabatic index of the gas.

Notation:

  1. \(R\) (R) is molar_gas_constant.

Conditions:

  1. The gas is ideal.

Links:

  1. Wikipedia.

isobaric_molar_heat_capacity¶

molar_heat_capacity at constant pressure.

Symbol:

c_pm

Latex:

\(c_{p, \text{m}}\)

Dimension:

energy/(amount_of_substance*temperature)

adiabatic_index¶

adiabatic_index, or Heat capacity ratio of the gas.

Symbol:

gamma

Latex:

\(\gamma\)

Dimension:

dimensionless

law¶

c_pm = R * gamma / (gamma - 1)

Latex:
\[c_{p, \text{m}} = \frac{R \gamma}{\gamma - 1}\]
