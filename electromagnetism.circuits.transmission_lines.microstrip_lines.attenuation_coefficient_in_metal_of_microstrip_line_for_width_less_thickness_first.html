Attenuation coefficient in microstrip metal when thickness is greater than width times \(2 \pi\)¶

Under the conditions described below, the attenuation coefficient of the microstrip metal can be calculated from the surge impedance of the line, the surface resistance of the metal, the effective permittivity of the substrate, and the physical dimensions of the system.

Conditions:

  1. \(h \ge 2 \pi w_\text{eff}\).

Here, \(h\) is substrate thickness, and \(w_\text{eff}\) is effective width of the microstrip.

law¶

alpha = 1.38 * R_s / (h * Z_S) * (32 - (w_eff / h)^2) / (32 + (w_eff / h)^2) * (1 + h / w_eff * (1 + 1.25 / pi * t / w + 1.25 / pi * log(4 * pi * w / t)))

Latex:
\[\alpha = \frac{1.38 R_\text{s}}{h Z_\text{S}} \frac{32 - \left(\frac{w_\text{eff}}{h}\right)^{2}}{32 + \left(\frac{w_\text{eff}}{h}\right)^{2}} \left(1 + \frac{h}{w_\text{eff}} \left(1 + \frac{1.25}{\pi} \frac{t}{w} + \frac{1.25}{\pi} \log \left( \frac{4 \pi w}{t} \right)\right)\right)\]
