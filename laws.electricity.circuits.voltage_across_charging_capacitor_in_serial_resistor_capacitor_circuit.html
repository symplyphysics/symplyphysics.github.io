Voltage across charging capacitor in serial resistor-capacitor circuit¶

A series circuit containing only a resistor, a capacitor, a switch and a constant DC source of voltage is known as a charging circuit. The capacitor is initially uncharged while the switch is open. The switch is closed at \(t = 0\). Afterwards, the capacitor begins to charge and the voltage across it rises while the voltage across the resistor begins to drop. Note that the voltage across the capacitor never reaches the voltage of the source.

Conditions:

  1. The circuit is a DC one.
